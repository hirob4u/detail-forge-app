# V1.1 Product Vision -- Supply Cabinet

**Status:** Documented, deferred to v1.1
**Date:** March 2026
**Decision owner:** Ben Foran / Foranware

---

## The Problem With V1.0 Supply Tracking

The original v1.0 scope included supply cost tracking as a core feature. The intent was to track product costs per job and feed that data into cost analytics to show true job profitability.

The problem is the data model required to do this accurately:

- Detailer enters every product they use, including unit cost and container size
- Detailer estimates usage per job per service type
- System calculates cost per job based on usage estimates

This is a significant data entry burden upfront and the output is a rough guess at best. Detailers are operators, not accountants. A detailer doing five cars a week is not going to spend an hour entering 40 products into a database and estimating milliliters of compound per panel. The feature would go unused and create the impression that DetailForge requires a lot of setup to get value.

The original scope was removed from v1.0 and replaced with a coming soon shell.

---

## The Right Problem to Solve

What detailers actually feel every day is not "what did my supplies cost on that last job?" It is:

- Running out of a compound mid-correction job on a customer's car
- Discovering the ceramic coating they need is out of stock the morning of an appointment
- Buying a duplicate of something they already had because they forgot what was in the cabinet
- Not knowing what to use for an unusual job because their usual product isn't right for it

The supply cabinet solves the operational problem -- never run out, always know what you have, use the right product for the job -- rather than the accounting problem. The accounting problem (true cost per job) is still worth solving but it can be approximated at a coarser level without per-product tracking.

## The Supply Cabinet Vision

### Phase 1 -- Cabinet Setup Via Photo

The detailer builds their cabinet by photographing product bottles. They do not type anything.

Claude Vision reads the label and identifies:
- Product name
- Brand
- Category (compound, polish, coating, cleaner, dressing, pad, towel, other)
- Approximate container size if visible

The cabinet entry is created automatically. The detailer taps to confirm or make a quick correction if the label was misread. For products the model cannot confidently identify -- a partially obscured label, a private label product, a very new release -- it asks a single clarifying question: "This looks like a polish or compound -- which fits better?" One tap to confirm. No forms.

This is the same Vision API infrastructure already built for vehicle photo assessment. The use case is simpler and more reliable because product labels are designed to be read. A detailer can photograph their entire shelf in 10 minutes and have a complete cabinet.

**What the cabinet stores per product:**
- Product name and brand
- Category
- Photo of the bottle (taken during setup, stored in R2)
- Current stock status: Full / Getting Low / Out
- Date added
- Date last marked low or out

Stock status is manually updated with a single tap. DetailForge does not attempt to track usage automatically -- that requires the data entry burden we are eliminating.

### Phase 2 -- Job Recommendations From the Cabinet

When a job is created with a defined service scope, DetailForge surfaces the relevant products from the detailer's own cabinet.

Not generic recommendations. Their actual products.

Example -- a paint correction job on a 2012 sedan:

> "For a clear coat correction job on this vehicle you'll typically need a cutting compound, a finishing polish, cutting and finishing pads, and a paint sealant or coating. From your cabinet:
>
> Compound: Menzerna Heavy Cut 400
> Finishing Polish: Chemical Guys VSS
> Pads: Rupes Yellow Cutting, Rupes Blue Finishing
> Sealant: CarPro Reload
>
> You don't have an iron decontamination spray in your cabinet. This is recommended before correction on a vehicle this age."

The recommendation engine maps service type to product categories and then looks up the detailer's cabinet for matches. It does not recommend specific brands or products the detailer doesn't own -- it works with what they have and flags missing categories as a heads-up, not a hard requirement.

If the detailer has multiple products in the same category -- two different compounds for example -- it surfaces both and lets them choose which to use for that job. The choice is logged against the job for future reference.

**This recommendation data has long-term value.** Over time DetailForge learns which products a detailer reaches for on which job types. This creates a foundation for smarter recommendations and eventual reorder intelligence without requiring any additional input from the detailer.

### Phase 3 -- Reorder Reminders

DetailForge tracks when products are marked low or out and uses job volume to estimate when a reorder is due.

**How the coarse usage model works:**

The detailer sets their approximate job frequency in settings -- rough categories are sufficient:

- Light (1-4 jobs per month)
- Moderate (5-10 jobs per month)
- Active (10-20 jobs per month)
- High volume (20+ jobs per month)

DetailForge counts completed jobs since a product was last marked Full and applies typical yield estimates by product category and job frequency to estimate when a product might be running low.

Example: A compound marked Full with an active detailer doing 15 correction jobs per month would trigger a "might be running low" nudge after 8-10 jobs. Not precise -- but good enough to prompt the detailer to check before they run out.

**Reorder nudge surfaces:**

- Dashboard notification badge on the Supplies nav item
- In-app alert when opening a job that uses a product currently marked Low or Out: "Your Menzerna 400 is running low -- consider reordering before this job."
- Optional email digest -- weekly summary of products that are low or may need reordering based on upcoming job load

**The nudge is a suggestion, not a workflow.** DetailForge does not integrate with suppliers, does not generate purchase orders, and does not track purchase history in v1.1. The goal is to interrupt the "discovered mid-job that I'm out" failure mode, not to automate procurement.

---

### What Stays Out of Scope for V1.1

The following are explicitly deferred beyond v1.1:

**Per-job cost calculation from supply usage:** Requires accurate usage rates per product per service type. Too much data entry burden and too many variables to be reliable. May be revisited when we have enough job and product data to make reasonable inferences automatically.

**Supplier integrations:** No connections to Detailed Image, The Rag Company, Chemical Guys, or other suppliers. No purchase history, no one-click reorder. This requires supplier API relationships and a more complex data model than v1.1 warrants.

**Automatic usage deduction:** No attempt to subtract estimated product usage when a job is marked complete. Too imprecise to be trustworthy and would require the detailer to verify and correct constantly.

**Price tracking and cost analytics:** Deferred until there is enough adoption to understand how detailers actually want to see cost data. A simple monthly supplies budget field in settings (enter what you spend per month) is sufficient for v1.1 cost estimation in analytics.

**Product recommendations outside the cabinet:** No brand recommendations for products the detailer doesn't own. This is a business decision as much as a technical one -- any brand recommendation implies an endorsement and creates support surface. The cabinet works with what the detailer already uses.

## V1.1 Blueprint Sequence

When supply cabinet work begins, the Blueprint sequence is:

1. **Schema** -- products table, cabinet_entries table with stock status, job_products junction for recommendation logging
2. **Cabinet setup** -- photo capture flow using existing R2 infrastructure, Vision API label identification, cabinet management UI
3. **Job recommendations** -- service-to-category mapping, cabinet lookup, recommendation UI on job detail page
4. **Reorder reminders** -- coarse usage model, low stock flags, dashboard nudges, optional email digest

Each Blueprint depends on the previous. Do not begin Blueprint 3 until Blueprint 2 is merged and tested with real products.

---

## Trigger for V1.1 Work

Begin supply cabinet Blueprints when:

- All six v1.0 core features are complete
- The landing page is live
- At least one real detailer is using the product actively
- The baseline-first estimate architecture (see separate v1.1 decision doc) is scoped or in progress

The supply cabinet and baseline estimate are independent v1.1 tracks and can be built in parallel by separate agents once the v1.0 foundation is stable.

---

## Why This Is Better Than the Original Scope

The original supply cost tracking required a lot from the detailer to get anything out. The supply cabinet gives value immediately -- the first photo scan of a product label is useful on its own. Every additional photo makes the recommendations better. The reorder reminders add value passively without any additional input.

The shift from accounting tool to operational tool is the right product instinct. Detailers are craftspeople. The tools they reach for are the ones that get out of their way and make the job easier. A supply cabinet that knows what they have and reminds them before they run out is a tool they will use. A cost tracker that requires weekly data entry is a tool that will be abandoned.

No other detailing software does AI-assisted cabinet setup from product photos. This is a genuine differentiator and it is built on infrastructure DetailForge already has.
