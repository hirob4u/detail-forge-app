**Status:** Documented, ready for Blueprint execution
**Date:** March 2026
**Decision owner:** Ben Foran / Foranware

**Companion to:** V1.1 Methodology Engine, V1.1 Baseline Architecture, V1.1 Supply Cabinet

---

## Summary

This document identifies six operational gaps in the v1.0 job dashboard that prevent it from being the tool a detailer reaches for every morning. The gaps share a common root cause: the dashboard is organized around **data** (here is everything we know about this job) rather than **action** (here is what you need to do right now to make money).

The v1.1 docs solved the intelligence problem (methodology engine, baselines, supply cabinet). This document solves the operations problem: how the detailer's workday flows through the tool.

---

## Part 1: Current State (v1.0 Job Dashboard)

The job detail page is a vertical stack of cards rendered identically regardless of stage:

1. Header (job ID, vehicle, stage badge, edit link)
2. Stage pipeline (visual dots, created through complete)
3. Two-column grid: PackagePricingCard + CustomerCard
4. CustomerNotesCard
5. PhotoViewer (filmstrip thumbnails, lightbox on click)
6. JobNotes (editable, auto-save)
7. AIConditionNotesCard (3-flag teaser, "view all" link)
8. AnalysisStatusPanel (polling, retry, stuck detection)
9. QC link (only when stage = qc)
10. StageControls (forward/backward transitions)
11. SocialExportPanel (only when stage = complete)
12. StageHistory (collapsible timeline)

The job list page is a filtered table of job cards grouped by stage tab.

**What works well:**
- AI analysis with real-time polling and retry logic
- Two-phase photo loading (metadata first, presigned URLs second)
- Quote builder with AI-recommended line items and detailer adjustments
- Stage transitions with confirmation dialogs and audit trail
- QC checklist with before/after photo comparison
- Social export with watermark and plate blocking

**What is missing:** Everything that connects the features to the detailer's business day.

## Part 2: Six Operational Gaps

### Gap 1: The Job Detail Page Is Stage-Blind

The page renders the same card stack whether a job is in `created` (needs quote), `sent` (waiting on customer), `inProgress` (being worked), or `qc` (needs after photos). The detailer must scroll through 12 sections and figure out which cards are relevant.

A detailer with 6 active jobs at different stages cannot glance at a job and know what to do. They have to read the stage badge, then mentally map it to which section matters, then scroll to find that section.

**What the detailer actually needs per stage:**

| Stage | Primary action | Supporting context |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| created | Review AI assessment, build quote | Photos, condition flags, customer intents |
| quoted | Send quote to customer | Quote summary, customer email, preview |
| sent | Wait (or follow up if aging) | Days since sent, customer contact info |
| approved | Schedule and start work | Quote details, supply recommendations |
| inProgress | Complete work | Quote details, detailer notes |
| qc | Upload after photos, run checklist | Before photos for comparison, checklist |
| complete | Export social content, archive | Before/after photos, social export tools |

The page should reshape around the current stage, surfacing the primary action prominently and collapsing everything else behind progressive disclosure.

### Gap 2: The AI Assessment Is Buried

The AI analyzes 13 areas, scores conditions, flags issues, and recommends services with pricing. This is the core value proposition -- the thing that saves the detailer 15 minutes of manual inspection notes.

On the job detail page, this appears as:
- A slim card showing **at most 3 flag titles** at the bottom of the scroll
- A "View all condition notes" link that navigates to the review page
- No photos tied to findings
- No condition summary visible without clicking through

The assessment should be front and center on `created` stage jobs. The detailer should see the full condition summary and feel "this thing already did half my inspection."

### Gap 3: Photos Are Disconnected From Findings

The AI says "moderate swirl marks on driver side." The photo of the driver side is in a filmstrip 400px away. The detailer has to mentally cross-reference findings with thumbnails, clicking into a lightbox to see detail.

For a detailer evaluating whether to trust the AI's recommendation, this friction breaks the assessment flow. Photos should appear next to the findings they relate to, or at minimum be linked so clicking a flag jumps to the relevant photo.

### Gap 4: No Revenue Visibility

The job list shows filtered cards. It does not answer:
- "How much approved work do I have this week?"
- "How many quotes are waiting on customer response?"
- "What did I complete this month?"
- "What's my quote-to-approval conversion rate?"

These are the numbers that make a detailer open the app every morning. Without them, the dashboard is a task list, not a business tool.

### Gap 5: Job Aging Is Invisible

A quote sent 5 days ago with no response looks identical to one sent 2 hours ago. There is no visual indicator of aging, no nudge to follow up, no way to see at a glance which jobs need attention.

For the detailer, a stale quote is lost revenue. The tool should surface this proactively.

### Gap 6: Customer Communication Is Buried

Sending a quote is a button inside StageControls, a generic stage transition panel at the bottom of the page. There is:
- No preview of what the customer will see
- No tracking of whether the customer opened the quote
- No follow-up reminder
- No easy way to resend with a personal note

The quote send is the revenue moment -- the action that turns a lead into a paying job. It deserves first-class treatment, not a button in a utility panel.

## Part 3: Design Principles

### Principle 1: Stage-Aware Layout
The job detail page should feel different at each stage because the detailer's task is different at each stage. Not different pages -- the same URL, but with the layout reshaping around the current action.

### Principle 2: Intelligence Forward
AI findings, recommendations, and flags should be visible without extra clicks. The detailer should see the AI's work as soon as they open a job, not after navigating to a sub-page.

### Principle 3: Revenue Is the Metric
The dashboard exists to help the detailer make money. Pipeline value, conversion rate, aging quotes, and weekly earnings are not analytics features -- they are the reason to open the app.

### Principle 4: Evidence-Linked Findings
Any AI assertion about vehicle condition should be one click from the photo that supports it. Findings without evidence are opinions. Findings with linked photos are tools.

### Principle 5: Communication Is a Feature
Quote delivery, customer follow-up, and response tracking are not stage transitions. They are the revenue conversion layer and deserve dedicated UI treatment.

## Part 4: Architecture

### 4.1 Stage-Aware Job Detail Layout

The job detail page becomes a stage-aware layout. A single server component reads `job.stage` and renders a stage-specific composition of existing components.

**No new components required for Phase 1.** The existing cards (PackagePricingCard, CustomerCard, PhotoViewer, AIConditionNotesCard, StageControls, etc.) are rearranged and given different prominence based on stage.

**Layout structure:**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Header (always: vehicle, stage, pipeline)  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  PRIMARY ACTION ZONE (stage-specific)       │
│  - The one thing the detailer needs to do   │
│  - CTA button for that action               │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  CONTEXT PANEL (stage-specific)             │
│  - Supporting info for the current action   │
│  - Photos, flags, quote summary, etc.       │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  EVERYTHING ELSE (collapsible)              │
│  - Full details, history, notes, etc.       │
│  - Available but not competing for attention│
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Stage compositions:**

**created (AI processing or complete):**
- Primary: AI condition summary with linked photos + "Review & Build Quote" CTA
- Context: Customer card, intents, customer notes
- Collapsed: Stage history, detailer notes

**quoted (quote finalized, not yet sent):**
- Primary: Quote summary + "Send to Customer" CTA with preview
- Context: Customer contact info, quote line items
- Collapsed: AI assessment, photos, history

**sent (waiting on customer):**
- Primary: Aging indicator ("Sent 3 days ago") + "Follow Up" CTA
- Context: Quote summary, customer contact, last sent date
- Collapsed: Full assessment, photos, history

**approved (customer accepted):**
- Primary: "Start Work" CTA + quote details
- Context: Supply recommendations (v1.1), customer notes, photos for reference
- Collapsed: Assessment, history

**inProgress (work underway):**
- Primary: "Move to QC" CTA + detailer notes (editable, prominent)
- Context: Quote line items as work checklist, photos for reference
- Collapsed: Customer info, assessment, history

**qc (quality check):**
- Primary: QC checklist + after-photo upload (inline, not separate page)
- Context: Before photos for side-by-side comparison
- Collapsed: Quote details, customer info, history

**complete:**
- Primary: Social export tools (inline) + "Archive" option
- Context: Before/after photo comparison, job summary
- Collapsed: Full history, quote details

### 4.2 Revenue Dashboard

A new summary section at the top of the jobs list page. Not a separate page -- it lives above the stage filter tabs.

**Metrics (server-computed):**

| Metric | Source | Display |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Pipeline value | SUM(finalQuote.totalPrice) WHERE stage IN (quoted, sent) | Dollar amount |
| Approved this week | SUM(finalQuote.totalPrice) WHERE stage = approved AND approvedAt >= weekStart | Dollar amount |
| Completed this week | COUNT(*) WHERE stage = complete AND completedAt >= weekStart | Job count + total |
| Awaiting response | COUNT(*) WHERE stage = sent | Count with aging breakdown |
| Conversion rate | approved / (quoted + sent + approved) trailing 30 days | Percentage |

**No new tables required.** All data derives from existing `jobs` fields: `stage`, `finalQuote`, `stageHistory` (timestamps for stage transitions).

Schema addition: index on `stage` + `organizationId` if not already present for efficient aggregation.

### 4.3 Job Aging Indicators

Visual indicators on job cards and the job detail page showing time-in-stage:

- **Green (fresh):** < 24 hours in current stage
- **Amber (aging):** 24-72 hours in current stage (for `sent` and `quoted` only)
- **Red (stale):** > 72 hours in current stage (for `sent` and `quoted` only)

Source: `stageHistory` array, last entry timestamp vs. now.

For `sent` stage specifically: show "Sent X days ago" with follow-up CTA when aging > 48 hours.

### 4.4 Quote Communication Enhancement

Elevate quote sending from a stage transition button to a dedicated experience:

- **Quote preview:** Before sending, show the detailer what the customer will see (rendered quote card)
- **Personal note:** Optional message included with the quote email
- **Send tracking:** Record `quoteSentAt` (already exists), surface it prominently on `sent` stage
- **Follow-up nudge:** When a `sent` job ages > 48 hours, show a "Send Follow-Up" CTA that pre-fills a gentle reminder email

Note: Full customer portal (Track B from v1.1 methodology engine doc) is a separate effort. This enhancement improves the detailer's side of the existing email-based quote flow.

### 4.5 Photo-Finding Linkage

Connect AI findings to their source photos:

- AI assessment flags already include area context (the AI scores by area: hood, driver-side, etc.)
- Photos already have `area` metadata from structured capture
- Link each flag to photos matching its area
- In the condition summary, show a thumbnail next to each finding
- Click the thumbnail to open the lightbox at that photo

No schema changes. Matching is done client-side by comparing `flag.area` (or inferring from flag title/description) to `photo.area`.

## Part 5: What Stays Out of Scope

**Customer portal / digital quote approval (Track B):** Requires schema for quote tokens, public routes, and customer authentication. This is a v1.2 Track B effort, not an operations layer change.

**Stripe / payments:** Depends on customer portal. Not addressed here.

**Scheduling / calendar:** Separate product surface. Not a jobs dashboard feature.

**Analytics dashboard (deep):** Conversion funnels, profitability analysis, and trend charts are a v1.3 concern. The revenue summary metrics here are the minimum viable business visibility.

**Email open tracking:** Requires email infrastructure changes (tracking pixels, webhook receivers). Deferred. The aging indicator provides a passive signal that is good enough for v1.2.

**Push notifications:** Mobile push for aging jobs, new customer submissions, etc. Deferred until mobile usage patterns are understood.

## Part 6: Blueprint Sequence

### Phase 1: Stage-Aware Layout (v1.0 polish -- no new infrastructure)

#### Blueprint OPS-1 -- feat/stage-aware-job-detail
Reshape the job detail page into stage-aware zones. Reuse all existing components. No new APIs, no schema changes.
**Branch:** `feat/stage-aware-job-detail`
**Depends on:** Nothing

### Phase 2: Revenue Visibility (v1.2 core)

#### Blueprint OPS-2 -- feat/revenue-dashboard-summary
Add revenue summary metrics above the job list. Server-computed aggregations from existing data.
**Branch:** `feat/revenue-dashboard-summary`
**Depends on:** Nothing

#### Blueprint OPS-3 -- feat/job-aging-indicators
Add time-in-stage visual indicators to job cards and job detail page. Amber/red aging for quoted/sent stages.
**Branch:** `feat/job-aging-indicators`
**Depends on:** Nothing

### Phase 3: Communication Enhancement (v1.2)

#### Blueprint OPS-4 -- feat/quote-send-experience
Quote preview, personal note field, and follow-up CTA for aging sent quotes.
**Branch:** `feat/quote-send-experience`
**Depends on:** OPS-1 (stage-aware layout provides the `sent` stage primary zone)

### Phase 4: Intelligence Presentation (v1.2)

#### Blueprint OPS-5 -- feat/photo-finding-linkage
Connect AI flags to source photos by area. Thumbnails next to findings. Click-to-lightbox.
**Branch:** `feat/photo-finding-linkage`
**Depends on:** OPS-1 (stage-aware layout provides the `created` stage primary zone)

```
FIRST:    OPS-1  (Stage-Aware Layout -- unlocks all other OPS work)
PARALLEL: OPS-2  (Revenue Dashboard)
          OPS-3  (Job Aging)
```

OPS-1 is the keystone. It creates the stage-specific zones where every subsequent Blueprint places its work. Without it, revenue indicators, aging badges, and communication enhancements have no natural home.

## Part 8: Why This Matters

The v1.1 methodology engine makes the AI smarter. The supply cabinet makes operations smoother. The baseline architecture makes estimates more reliable. But none of that matters if the detailer opens the dashboard and sees a wall of cards that doesn't tell them what to do next.

The business operations layer is the connective tissue. It turns six powerful features into a workflow. It answers the detailer's real questions: "What do I need to do right now?" and "How's my week looking?"

A detailer who opens DetailForge and immediately sees $2,400 in approved work, 2 quotes aging past 48 hours, and a clear next action on each job is a detailer who reaches for the tool every morning. That is the product.
