## Part 3: Two Fundamental Architecture Problems

### Problem 1: The AI is playing the wrong role

Current flow:
```
Customer photos --> AI assesses (as the expert) --> Detailer reviews/corrects
```

The 423-line prompt encodes DetailForge's generic knowledge. Every org gets the same AI -- same pricing ranges, same service names, same correction protocols. A ceramic coating specialist and a mobile wash operator get identical behavior.

The detailer is the expert. The AI should be their trained apprentice, not a generic assessor.

### Problem 2: Interior is a second-class citizen

The assessment system was designed around exterior paint correction:

| Dimension | Current Coverage |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Exterior scoring** | 3 of 5 dimensions (paint condition, scratch severity, contamination) |
| **Interior scoring** | 1 of 5 dimensions (everything crammed into one score) |
| **Exterior photos** | 5 required + 3 optional = 8 shots |
| **Interior photos** | 3 required + 2 optional = 5 shots |
| **Prompt lines** | ~120 lines for exterior paint; ~40 lines for interior |

A huge segment of the market -- mobile detailers, family car owners, fleet cleaning, rideshare drivers -- primarily needs interior work. A customer whose kids destroyed the back seat, whose dog lives in the cargo area, who spilled coffee on the center console, and who smokes in the car gets the same depth of AI assessment as someone with a single paint swirl.

Interior needs the same multi-dimensional scoring as exterior:
- **Seats/upholstery** (staining severity, material condition, wear)
- **Carpet/floor** (soiling, pet hair, staining, moisture damage)
- **Dashboard/console/hard surfaces** (dust buildup, sticky residue, UV damage, piano black condition)
- **Door panels** (pocket debris, armrest wear, speaker grille condition)
- **Headliner** (staining, delamination risk, sagging)
- **Odor indicators** (visual evidence: smoke residue, mold, pet, food)
- **Trunk/cargo** (soiling, pet hair, cargo damage)

### The right architecture solves both:
```
Detailer methodology (their rules, their focus) --> AI applies those rules to photos --> Equal depth interior + exterior
```

## Part 4: The Detailer Methodology Engine

### Three-Layer Prompt Architecture

**Layer 1: Foundation (DetailForge-maintained)**
Scoring rubric, photo analysis technique, output schema, professional writing standard, safety guardrails (non-correctable damage, headliner delamination risk, clear coat failure rules).

**Layer 2: Detailer Methodology (per-org)**
Each detailer defines:
- **Service menu** -- Their services, descriptions, pricing tiers by vehicle category, trigger conditions
- **Inspection focus** -- Interior-weighted, exterior-weighted, or balanced. Controls which scoring dimensions get the most AI attention.
- **Pricing rules** -- Base prices by vehicle category, condition multipliers, minimums, surcharges
- **Correction protocols** -- Their approach at each severity level, in their language
- **Product preferences** -- Their products for each application (feeds supply cabinet)
- **Quality standards** -- What "complete" means for each service

**Layer 3: Learning (auto-generated from behavior)**
- Price adjustments the detailer consistently makes --> pricing calibration
- Services consistently added/removed --> recommendation weights
- Feedback patterns ("needs work" on specific error types) --> accuracy refinement
- No detailer action required. System watches and learns.

### How Detailers Teach the System

**Guided onboarding** (Day 1): Structured interview capturing business type, service menu, pricing, vehicle categories.

**Rule builder** (Ongoing): Settings UI for adding/editing rules.

**Behavioral learning** (Automatic): System aggregates corrections over 20+ jobs and adjusts methodology layer silently.

### Dynamic Prompt Assembly

```
Foundation prompt (Layer 1)
+ "This detailer's service menu: [...]"
+ "This detailer's pricing: [...]"
+ "This detailer's priorities: [...]"
+ "Based on historical patterns: [...]"
= Custom prompt unique to this org
```

Falls back to current monolithic prompt if no methodology exists (backward compatible).

## Part 8: Why This Is Unprecedented

No tool in any industry lets a domain expert teach an AI their personal methodology and have it applied consistently at scale while continuously improving from their corrections.

The moat: A detailer with 200 jobs through DetailForge has an AI inspector uniquely calibrated to THEIR expertise, pricing, market, and standards. That calibration cannot transfer. A competitor offers a generic AI. DetailForge offers THEIR AI.
